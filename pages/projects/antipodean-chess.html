Antipodean Chess

A spin on the classic take of chess.

Rather than taking place on a board, this version uses a sphere.

There are two featured modes. Sphere and Antipodean.

In sphere mode, the rules are the same as standard chess. However, columns A and H are considered adjacent.

Antipodean mode introduces many new mechanics which are laid out below.

Rules

  • Axial Squares
    • There are two axial squares. Alpha on the top of the sphere and Omega on the bottom of the sphere.
    • Pieces located on these squares are allowed to begin their movements in the direction of any adjacent tiles.
  • Inversion
    • An alternate board is located on the inside of the sphere. One can switch between which is in view freely.
  • Starting Pieces/Positions
    • There are four knights for each team on either side of the board.
    • The king is located on the outer board on an axial tile while the queen is located on the same axial tile on the inner board.
  • Push/Send and Pull/Retrieve
    • The queen can send pieces on her board to the relatively inverse board, placing them on the same square of the board inverse to the queen.
      • This can only take place if the other side is empty.
      • A queen may not push herself.
      • Another queen may push a different queen.
    • The king can retrieve pieces from the relatively inverse board,Â  placing them on the same square of the king's board.
      • This can only take place if the other side is empty.
      • The King may not retrieve himself.
      • If a queen is selected and located on the same square as the king on the board inverse to the king, they may be "swapped" in a single turn. This allows the king and queen to be placed on the same square of the board previously considered relatively inverse.
    • Either of these actions will end the turn.
    • If done on a pawn, it will not count as the pawn's first move.
